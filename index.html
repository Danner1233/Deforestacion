THE INTERNATIONAL TROPICAL TIMBER COUNCIL

Web Version on Deforestation International Tropical Timber Council The International Tropical Timber Council (ITTC) is working to reduce deforestation and forest degradation during its 41st session in Yokohama, Japan. The Council, governing the International Tropical Timber Organization (ITTO), promotes sustainable forest management and the trade of responsibly produced timber. Key objectives include fighting illegal deforestation and wildlife trafficking while expanding the global market for sustainable timber. This session will address issues such as law enforcement in Brazil and Ecuador, the inclusion of ramin in CITES Appendix II, and sustainable forest management in the Congo Basin. Side events will focus on community rights and Japan's timber procurement policies.

CURRENT CHALLENGES

Current Situation on Deforestation In 2024, deforestation remains critical, especially in the Amazon and Southeast Asia. Brazil and Indonesia account for nearly half of global tropical deforestation, with Brazil losing approximately 1.7 million hectares per year, turning the Amazon into a net carbon emitter. In contrast, Colombia has significantly reduced primary forest loss, while Bolivia faces increased deforestation due to agricultural fires. Agriculture and products such as soy and palm oil remain the biggest drivers of deforestation, and corporate commitments to reduce it have proven insufficient.

KEY SOLUTIONS

Solutions to Deforestation Sustainable Forest Management: Encourages responsible logging practices and reforestation programs to restore degraded areas. Protected Areas: Expands natural reserves with strict laws to prevent illegal logging and land conversion. Sustainable Agriculture: Techniques like agroforestry integrate trees and crops, reducing the need for deforestation. Economic Incentives: Programs like Payment for Ecosystem Services (PES) reward forest preservation efforts. Education and Public Awareness: Raising awareness helps foster sustainable consumer behaviors.
