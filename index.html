Deforestation is the process of removing or destroying forests and woodlands, primarily to make way for agriculture, livestock grazing, urbanization, or resource extraction. This practice involves the cutting, burning, or clearing of trees and vegetation. Forests are vital for ecosystem balance as they regulate climate, conserve biodiversity, and maintain the water cycle. When large areas are deforested, stored carbon dioxide (CO₂) is released, contributing to climate change. Additionally, deforestation can lead to soil erosion and negatively impact communities that rely on these ecosystems for their livelihoods.
